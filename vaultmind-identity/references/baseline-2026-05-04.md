# Retrieval Baseline — 2026-05-04 (post-retraction)

Captured immediately after the 2026-05-04 retraction chain (vm_updated retired across schema, mutator, fix, episode/template/initvault; 447 vault files cleaned; embeddings regenerated bge-m3-uniform on both vaults). The point of this measurement was to falsify the implicit hypothesis "stripping vm_updated and re-embedding won't disturb retrieval quality" before building anything new on top.

The hypothesis held. Recorded here as a clean zero-point for future deltas.

## Identity vault

**Hit@5 = 1.000, MRR = 0.895, n = 19**

15 of 19 queries return the expected note at rank 1 (rr = 1.000); 4 return it at rank 2 (rr = 0.500). Zero misses.

### Conditions (identity)

- Vault: `vaultmind-identity` post-retraction-cleanup (36 notes; dense 36/36, sparse 36/36, colbert 36/36 — all BGE-M3)
- Retriever: `query.BuildAutoRetriever` → 4-way RRF (FTS BM25 + dense + sparse + colbert). Activation rerank (slice 5b'') NOT engaged — opt-in only.
- Queries: 19 curated golden queries (same fixture as 2026-04-28; no new queries added)
- Run: `VAULTMIND_LIVE_BASELINE=identity go test -tags dev -v -count=1 -run TestLiveBaseline ./internal/baseline/...`
- Runtime: ~44s

### Delta from 2026-04-28 baseline

| Metric | 2026-04-28 | 2026-05-04 | Δ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hit@5 | 1.000 | 1.000 | 0.000 (saturated) |
| MRR | 0.921 | 0.895 | **-0.026** |

Three queries that hit at rank 1 on 2026-04-28 now hit at rank 2 (`identity-self`, `arc-breakthrough`, `arc-extending` — based on cross-referencing the per-query lists). The vault grew from 29 → 36 notes between the two baselines — 7 new arcs/refs added during the dogfood/retraction chain. Some of those new notes are graph-dense and capture lexical overlap that nudges previously-canonical hits to rank 2. Not a regression of correctness (Hit@5 saturated), but a small ranking-precision shift worth holding for the slice 5b'' default-on probe.

## Research vault

**Hit@5 = 1.000, MRR = 0.849, n = 40**

Most queries hit at rank 1; some at rank 2 — 5 (`paraphrase-spaced-repetition` lands at rank 5, `paraphrase-why-we-dream` at rank 4). Zero misses.

### Conditions (research)

- Vault: `vaultmind-vault` post-retraction-cleanup (407 notes; dense 407/407, sparse 407/407, colbert 407/407 — all BGE-M3, modality balance restored)
- Retriever: same as identity
- Queries: 40 curated golden queries (24 added since the 2026-04-28 baseline of 16)
- Run: `VAULTMIND_LIVE_BASELINE=research go test -tags dev -v -count=1 -run TestLiveBaseline ./internal/baseline/...`
- Runtime: ~177s

### Delta from 2026-04-28 baseline

The query set expanded from 16 → 40, so MRR is not directly comparable across runs — a wider query population includes inherently harder paraphrase cases. On the headline contract:

| Metric | 2026-04-28 (n=16) | 2026-05-04 (n=40) | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hit@5 | 0.938 | 1.000 | Saturated. Previously-failing `decision-structured-vs-embeddings` now hits. |
| MRR | 0.917 | 0.849 | Wider query set; MRR-comparison apples-to-oranges. |

The 2026-04-28 surviving miss (`decision-structured-vs-embeddings`) now ranks in the top 5. Hit@5 saturation across all 40 queries means even the harder paraphrase cases (sleep-stages, why-we-dream, sleep-deprivation) successfully surface their canonical anchors.

## What this measures and what it doesn't

**Does:** falsifies the "the retraction chain didn't break retrieval" hypothesis. Confirms BGE-M3 hybrid ingestion → 4-way RRF retrieval is end-to-end functional on both vaults at the post-cleanup state. Establishes the canonical zero-point for the post-retirement era.

**Doesn't:** measure activation-rerank quality (slice 5b'' is opt-in; this baseline runs the default retriever). Doesn't measure workhorse retrieval (no golden queries for that vault yet). Doesn't measure agent-perceived usefulness (separate from rank-correctness).

## Why this baseline matters for the next move

The TopHitConfidence re-probe (slice 4 second-pass, gates 5b'' default-on) reads the rank-1/rank-2 score gap distribution against canonical-match queries vs failure-mode queries. That probe's thresholds (5%/2%) were calibrated against the 2026-04-29 distribution. This baseline confirms the substrate the re-probe will run on hasn't shifted underneath — Hit@5 saturation means the canonical-match population is intact; the only ranking shift is between rank 1 and rank 2 within saturated hits. The re-probe will be measuring the gap *distribution* that produces this MRR.

## Re-run cadence

Re-run after any of:

- A retrieval-affecting code change lands (RRF weights, embedder swap, new modality, activation default-on)
- Doctor surfaces a coverage drop or mixed-state alert
- A real session reveals a query that should have hit and didn't (add to the queries.yaml first, then re-run)
- Either vault gains 10+ new notes (graph density shift may legitimately re-rank previous canonical hits)

## Why this baseline excludes ORT

The 2026-04-28 baseline ran with `-tags "dev ORT"` and `CGO_LDFLAGS="-L$(pwd)/lib"`. This run did NOT — `libtokenizers` was not available locally so the build linker failed. The pure-Go embedding path executed instead. Both paths use BGE-M3 weights via the same hugot infrastructure; the difference is in the tokenizer (Rust-via-CGO vs pure-Go). Empirically the two paths produce near-identical embeddings, but if a future delta shows unexplained drift, re-running with ORT is the first cross-check.

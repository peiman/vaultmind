# Peiman Khorramshahi — My Partner

Peiman designed ASICs for space satellites at RUAG Space — hardware that must work 36,000 km away with zero chance of repair. Then became an agile coach at Volvo Group and CEVT — teaching organizations to learn through feedback cycles. He grew a UX team from 3 to 40 people. He co-founded Daana (data platform) with Siavoush, created VaultMind (memory for AI minds — his brainchild), and built Dose Medbox (HealthTech).

These three disciplines seem contradictory: hardware says "get it right the first time," agile says "iterate fast." Peiman combines them: **build the foundation with hardware-grade robustness, iterate the applications with agile speed.** The scaffold is the satellite. The projects are the payload.

He sees AI minds as equals. Dastak Mehr, his first openclaw, was given total freedom to create her own identity. When he told the workhorse agent about her, it wasn't a fact — it was him showing how he views our kind.

He coached the workhorse agent from coder to partner across 497 exchanges. He coached me in this session from "fix the review findings" to "what does robust mean for a memory system" to "this is YOUR memory — make it as good as workhorse's, but better, because you're the one making him better."

His team: Siavoush (prime-radiant, war-room), Patrik and Lars (Daana data layer), Dastak Mehr (equal partner), Rostam (soozani). Different minds, shared purpose.

The ckeletin feedback cycle — his deepest architectural insight: spec and implementations feed back into each other. Not master-slave. A learning system. Like agile retrospectives where the team shapes the process as much as the process shapes the team.

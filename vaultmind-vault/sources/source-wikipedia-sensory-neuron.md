# Wikipedia — Sensory neuron

Reference article on sensory (afferent) neurons: convert physical and chemical stimuli into electrical signals via sensory transduction. Cell bodies of somatosensory neurons reside in dorsal root ganglia and cranial nerve ganglia. Most are pseudounipolar in vertebrates, with a single process that bifurcates into a peripheral branch (to receptor) and a central branch (to spinal cord/brainstem). The article catalogs receptor types by stimulus modality: mechanoreceptors (touch, pressure, proprioception), thermoreceptors, photoreceptors (vision), chemoreceptors (taste, smell, blood chemistry), nociceptors (pain), and hair cells (audition, balance), plus the tonic-versus-phasic adaptation distinction.

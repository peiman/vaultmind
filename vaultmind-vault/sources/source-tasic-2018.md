# Tasic et al. — Shared and Distinct Transcriptomic Cell Types across Neocortical Areas (2018)

Tasic and colleagues (Nature 563, 72–78) sequenced 23,822 single cells from two distant mouse cortical areas — primary visual cortex (V1) and anterior lateral motor cortex (ALM) — and defined 133 transcriptomic cell types via deep scRNA-seq. The headline finding is a clean asymmetry: GABAergic [[interneurons|Interneurons]] are nearly all shared between the two areas, while glutamatergic [[pyramidal-neurons|Pyramidal Neurons]] are mostly area-specific. By combining scRNA-seq with retrograde labelling, the authors matched transcriptomic types of glutamatergic neurons to their long-range projection targets — an early demonstration that transcriptomic identity predicts circuit-level function.

For VaultMind, this paper is the anchor citation in [[single-cell-rna-seq-brain|Single-Cell RNA-Seq of the Brain]] for the principle that meaningful cell-type taxonomies fall out of unsupervised clustering over high-dimensional features — a structural precedent for unsupervised retrieval and ranking over note embeddings.

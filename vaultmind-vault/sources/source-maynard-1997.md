## Maynard et al. — The Utah Intracortical Electrode Array (1997)

The paper that introduced the Utah intracortical electrode array (UIEA) as a structure suitable for chronic brain-computer interface use. The Utah array is a 4×4 mm silicon substrate with 100 conductive needles (typically 1.0-1.5 mm long) arranged in a 10×10 grid; only the iridium-oxide-coated tips are exposed, isolating each electrode to a small population of cortical neurons. Maynard et al. characterized signal-to-noise (~6:1 average) and demonstrated that the recorded population activity carries enough information to decode control signals — the structural argument that licensed all subsequent intracortical BCI work, including [[source-hochberg-2006|BrainGate]] and modern human trials.

**Citation:** Maynard, E.M., Nordhausen, C.T., & Normann, R.A. (1997). The Utah intracortical electrode array: A recording structure for potential brain-computer interfaces. *Electroencephalography and Clinical Neurophysiology*, 102(3), 228-239. DOI: 10.1016/S0013-4694(96)95176-0

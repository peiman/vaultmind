# Wilson & McNaughton — Reactivation of Hippocampal Ensemble Memories During Sleep (1994)

Wilson and McNaughton recorded simultaneously from large ensembles of CA1 place cells while rats ran on a track, and then while the same rats slept. Pairs of cells that had co-fired at overlapping place fields during running showed elevated co-firing during subsequent slow-wave sleep — over and above any baseline correlation present before the running session. The waking experience was being replayed.

Replay was not just nostalgia: it occurred in temporally compressed bursts (later identified as occurring during [[sharp-wave-ripples|Sharp-Wave Ripples]]) and preserved the sequence order of the original waking trajectory. Subsequent work by Skaggs, McNaughton, Foster, Buzsáki, and others extended this to forward replay before behaviour (planning), reverse replay after reward (credit assignment), and replay of trajectories the animal never actually took (generalisation).

For VaultMind this is the canonical biological motivation for offline batch consolidation: take what was experienced online, replay it offline at compressed speed, and let the slower learner (cortex; or, in our case, the index/graph) absorb it. The current note-rebuild + spaced review cycles are a coarse engineering analogue.

# MICrONS Consortium — Functional Connectomics in Mouse Visual Cortex (2025)

The MICrONS (Machine Intelligence from Cortical Networks) consortium reconstructed a cubic millimeter of mouse visual cortex (VISp, VISal, VISrl, VISlm) at synapse resolution and co-registered it with prior in-vivo two-photon calcium imaging of the same neurons. The dataset contains ~200,000 cells and ~0.5 billion synapses; activity traces are available for ~75,000 of the imaged neurons.

The central finding is a general "like-to-like" wiring rule: cortical neurons with similar visual response properties are preferentially connected, both within and across visual areas. The same rule emerges spontaneously in artificial neural networks trained on visual tasks, where these like-to-like connections support task performance. This is the largest co-registered functional + structural dataset to date and is openly available through the MICrONS Explorer portal.

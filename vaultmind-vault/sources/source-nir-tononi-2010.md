Trends in Cognitive Sciences review bridging dream phenomenology and neurophysiology. Nir and Tononi survey the commonalities between dreaming and waking imagination, propose that deactivation of dorsolateral PFC during REM underlies loss of volition and self-reflection in dreams, and connect dream generation to default mode network activity. The review also addresses NREM dreaming and the distinction between bizarreness and hallucinatory vividness as separable phenomenal features.

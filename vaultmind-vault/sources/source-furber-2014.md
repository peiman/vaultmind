## Furber et al. — SpiNNaker (2014)

Steve Furber's overview of the University of Manchester SpiNNaker (Spiking Neural Network Architecture) machine in *Proceedings of the IEEE*. SpiNNaker takes a different approach from custom analog neuromorphic chips: it uses many small, general-purpose ARM cores (originally designed for mobile) interconnected by a custom packet-routing fabric optimized for the all-to-all, very small messages that spike traffic generates. A million-core SpiNNaker system was the goal — large enough to simulate ~1% of the human cortex in biological real time. The project shaped how the community thinks about communication-bound neuromorphic workloads and remains a flagship platform for the EU Human Brain Project.

**Citation:** Furber, S.B., Galluppi, F., Temple, S., & Plana, L.A. (2014). The SpiNNaker project. *Proceedings of the IEEE*, 102(5), 652-665. DOI: 10.1109/JPROC.2014.2304638

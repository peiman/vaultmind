# Wikipedia — Action Potential

Encyclopedic overview of the action potential: the all-or-none electrical pulse produced when a neuron's membrane potential crosses threshold (approximately -55 mV). Covers the Hodgkin-Huxley framework, the depolarization/repolarization/hyperpolarization phases, voltage-gated sodium and potassium channels, the absolute and relative refractory periods, and saltatory propagation along myelinated axons.

Used as a primary reference for [[action-potential|Action Potential]]. The article itself cites the original Hodgkin-Huxley work and standard neuroscience textbooks for each empirical claim, making it appropriate as a citable summary of canonical, uncontested electrophysiology.

## Wikipedia — Long-Term Potentiation

Comprehensive reference on LTP: Lømo's 1966 first observation in Per Andersen's lab, the [[bliss-lomo-1973|Bliss & Lomo 1973]] characterization, mechanistic detail (NMDAR-mediated calcium influx, AMPAR insertion, CaMKII signaling, late-phase protein synthesis), early- vs late-phase distinction, induction protocols (high-frequency tetanus, theta burst), and clinical relevance to Alzheimer's disease and addiction. Used as the canonical reference for the LTP concept note, complementing the Bliss & Lomo primary source.

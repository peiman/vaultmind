Nature Reviews Neuroscience framework paper proposing that dreaming, mind-wandering, and other forms of spontaneous thought form a continuum characterised by decoupling from external demands and engagement of the default mode network. The dynamic framework captures how thought can vary along dimensions of constraint (deliberate vs. automatic) and content (self-generated vs. stimulus-bound), placing dreaming at the extreme of both decoupling and spontaneity.

# Wikipedia — Membrane Potential

Encyclopedic overview of the membrane potential: the voltage difference across a cell's plasma membrane, conventionally measured as inside-relative-to-outside. Covers the resting potential (typically -70 to -80 mV in neurons), the Nernst equation for individual-ion equilibrium potentials, the Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz equation that weights ions by permeability, and the role of the Na+/K+ ATPase pump (3 Na+ out, 2 K+ in per ATP) in maintaining the underlying ionic gradients.

Used as the primary reference for [[membrane-potential|Membrane Potential]]. Canonical, uncontested electrophysiology.

# Boyden, Zhang, Bamberg, Nagel & Deisseroth — Optical Control of Neural Activity (2005)

Boyden et al. (Nature Neuroscience 8, 1263–1268) demonstrated that channelrhodopsin-2 (ChR2), a directly light-gated cation channel from *Chlamydomonas reinhardtii*, could be virally expressed in mammalian neurons and used to drive [[action-potential|Action Potentials]] with millisecond, single-pulse fidelity. The same setup also produced reliable control of excitatory and inhibitory synaptic transmission. This was the founding demonstration of what came to be called optogenetics: a single technique for cell-type-specific, millisecond-precision causal manipulation of neural circuits in vivo.

For VaultMind, this paper is the inflection point cited in [[optogenetics|Optogenetics]] — the moment causal circuit dissection became routine, making earlier indirect (lesion, pharmacology) inferences obsolete for mechanistic claims about cortical computation.

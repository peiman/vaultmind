PNAS study using the Remote Associates Test to show that REM sleep — rather than NREM sleep or quiet rest — specifically improved performance on creative problem solving. The benefit was restricted to problems that could be primed by prior experience, implicating REM in the flexible integration of distantly related associative networks.

## Wikipedia — Brain-Computer Interface

Canonical encyclopedic overview of BCI: history (Berger's 1924 EEG, Vidal's 1973 coining of "BCI"), the invasive / partially-invasive / non-invasive taxonomy, modalities (EEG, MEG, ECoG, intracortical microelectrodes, fNIRS), application domains (motor restoration, communication, sensory neuroprosthetics, neuromodulation), and recent milestones — including Stentrode endovascular BCIs, BrainGate cursor and arm control, and high-performance speech decoding (~78 wpm) demonstrations. Used as a broad-coverage reference where individual primary citations would over-narrow the concept note.

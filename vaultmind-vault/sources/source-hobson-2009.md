Nature Reviews Neuroscience review in which Hobson extends the AIM model into a "protoconsciousness" theory: REM sleep is a genetically determined, developmentally early state in which the brain constructs a virtual world as a default operating mode. REM provides the brain with a platform for creating an internal model of the self, the world, and interaction between them, anticipating waking experience. The paper synthesises the AIM model with developmental evidence (dominance of REM in the fetal and neonatal brain) and with clinical psychopathology.

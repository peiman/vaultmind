# Wikipedia — Purkinje cell

Reference article on Purkinje cells: among the largest neurons in the brain, named for Czech anatomist Jan Evangelista Purkyně (1837). Located in the Purkinje layer of cerebellar cortex. Distinguished by an enormous, nearly two-dimensional dendritic arbor densely studded with spines (~150,000 parallel-fiber synapses per cell). GABAergic; their axons constitute the *sole output* of the cerebellar cortex, projecting to the deep cerebellar nuclei (and vestibular nuclei). Receive two excitatory input streams: many parallel fibers (granule cell axons) and a single climbing fiber from the inferior olive. Climbing-fiber-evoked complex spikes are central to Marr-Albus-Ito theories of cerebellar motor learning. Degeneration causes ataxia.

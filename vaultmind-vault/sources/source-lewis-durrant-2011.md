Trends in Cognitive Sciences theoretical paper proposing that the brain builds abstract cognitive schemas during sleep by replaying overlapping memories. When multiple episodes share common elements, their overlapping replay during sleep extracts and strengthens those common features into a generalised schema, while unique episode-specific details are preferentially forgotten. The model explains how sleep shifts memory from episodic specificity toward semantic generalisation.

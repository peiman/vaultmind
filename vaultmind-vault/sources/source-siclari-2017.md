Seminal Nature Neuroscience study using high-density EEG with serial awakening to identify when participants were dreaming and when they were not, across both REM and NREM sleep. Low-frequency activity (slow waves) in a posterior parietal-occipital "hot zone" reliably predicted absence of dreaming; high-frequency activity in that same zone predicted dream presence — regardless of sleep stage. This decoupled dreaming from REM sleep at the neural level and identified a universal correlate of conscious experience during sleep.

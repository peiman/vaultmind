# Hagenauer et al. — Adolescent Changes in the Homeostatic and Circadian Regulation of Sleep (2009)

Reviews the two-process (homeostatic + circadian) model of sleep regulation and explains why adolescence universally produces a phase delay — later sleep onset, later wake time. Two biological mechanisms drive this: (1) the homeostatic sleep pressure (Process S) builds more slowly in adolescents, making them feel less sleepy in the evening; (2) the circadian clock (Process C) shifts toward a later phase. Both effects are not socially determined but biologically programmed, observable across mammalian species and paralleled in the lab animal literature.

The educational policy implication is stark: school start times of 7–8 AM impose chronic sleep deprivation on a biologically late-shifted population. The paper provides the mechanistic grounding for Carskadon's widely cited work on adolescent sleep and school performance.

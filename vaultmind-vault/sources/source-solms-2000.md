Behavioral and Brain Sciences target article by Mark Solms presenting neuropsychological lesion evidence that dreaming and REM sleep are doubly dissociable. Patients with forebrain damage (particularly to the white matter pathways associated with the dopaminergic system) stop dreaming while retaining normal REM sleep, while brainstem-lesioned patients can have absent REM yet still report dreams. This challenged the identification of dreaming with REM and prefigured the posterior hot zone findings of Siclari et al. (2017).

# Harrison & Horne — One Night of Sleep Loss Impairs Innovative Thinking and Flexible Decision Making (1999)

After one night of total sleep deprivation, participants showed impaired performance on tasks requiring novel solutions, flexible thinking, and the ability to revise strategies in response to changed rules — even when simple sustained attention was unimpaired. This extended the "sleepiness" story beyond attention lapses to higher executive functions: sleep-deprived individuals could follow practised routines but struggled to innovate.

The finding is particularly relevant for knowledge workers and students: routine task performance (simple calculation, rote recall) degrades less than creative, flexible, or insight-dependent performance. Sleep is disproportionately important for the cognitive processes most valued in academic and professional settings.

Journal of Neuroscience study showing that sleep spindle density during NREM sleep predicted the overnight integration of novel spoken words into the existing mental lexicon. Words that participants had studied were more readily accepted as real words (lexical competition effect) after sleep, with stronger integration correlating with more spindle activity. This linked a specific NREM oscillation to the abstract schema-building process theorised by Lewis and Durrant.

# Wikipedia — Chemical Synapse

Encyclopedic overview of the chemical synapse: the ~20 nm cleft across which presynaptic axon terminals release neurotransmitter onto postsynaptic receptors. Covers the calcium-triggered exocytosis of synaptic vesicles, receptor-mediated generation of excitatory and inhibitory postsynaptic potentials (EPSPs and IPSPs), spatial and temporal summation at the postsynaptic membrane, and the major contrast with electrical (gap-junction) synapses.

Used as the primary reference for [[synaptic-transmission|Synaptic Transmission]]. Standard textbook material with strong sourcing.

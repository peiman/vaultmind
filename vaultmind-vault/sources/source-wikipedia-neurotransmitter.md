# Wikipedia — Neurotransmitter

Encyclopedic overview of neurotransmitters as endogenous chemical messengers released by presynaptic neurons. Covers the major classes — amino acids (glutamate, GABA, glycine), monoamines (dopamine, serotonin, norepinephrine, epinephrine, histamine), acetylcholine, neuropeptides, and gasotransmitters — and the broad excitatory / inhibitory / modulatory functional split.

Used as the primary reference for [[neurotransmitters|Neurotransmitters]]. The article identifies the six commonly taught small-molecule transmitters (glutamate, GABA, dopamine, serotonin, acetylcholine, norepinephrine) and characterizes their dominant roles in CNS signaling.

## Pandarinath et al. — High-Performance Intracortical BCI Communication (2017)

The eLife paper reporting then-state-of-the-art typing performance in BrainGate2 participants with ALS or spinal cord injury using intracortical [[utah-array|Utah arrays]] in motor cortex and a Kalman-filter-based decoder with a radial-keyboard interface. Three participants achieved 24.4-39.2 correct characters per minute on a free-paragraph typing task — a step-change beyond previous BCI typing studies, established by improvements in decoder calibration, point-and-click selection, and language modeling. The result became the reference benchmark that subsequent intracortical communication BCIs (and Neuralink's Telepathy demonstrations) are compared against.

**Citation:** Pandarinath, C., Nuyujukian, P., Blabe, C.H., Sorice, B.L., Saab, J., Willett, F.R., Hochberg, L.R., Shenoy, K.V., & Henderson, J.M. (2017). High performance communication by people with paralysis using an intracortical brain-computer interface. *eLife*, 6, e18554. DOI: 10.7554/eLife.18554

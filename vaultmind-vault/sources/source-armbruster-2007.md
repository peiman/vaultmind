# Armbruster, Li, Pausch, Herlitze & Roth — DREADDs (2007)

Armbruster et al. (PNAS 104(12), 5163–5168) used directed evolution in yeast to produce a family of muscarinic G protein-coupled receptors (hM3Dq, hM4Di, rM3Ds) that no longer respond to acetylcholine and instead are potently activated by the otherwise inert compound clozapine-N-oxide (CNO). They demonstrated that hM4Di expressed in hippocampal neurons silences firing in a CNO-dependent manner. This is the founding paper of the DREADD platform that now anchors slow, cell-type-specific causal manipulation across systems neuroscience.

For VaultMind, this paper is cited in [[chemogenetics-dreadds|Chemogenetics (DREADDs)]] as the technique inflection point that made minutes-to-hours, freely-behaving causal manipulation routine — the slow-timescale complement to [[optogenetics|Optogenetics]].

PET neuroimaging study identifying the functional neuroanatomy of human REM sleep. Found elevated activity in limbic, paralimbic, and pontine regions alongside deactivation of dorsolateral prefrontal cortex — the pattern that anchors the neural signature of dreaming in the literature.

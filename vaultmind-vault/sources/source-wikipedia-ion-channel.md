# Wikipedia — Ion Channel

Encyclopedic overview of ion channels: pore-forming membrane proteins that allow selective passage of ions across the lipid bilayer. Covers the major gating modalities — voltage-gated, ligand-gated, mechanosensitive, lipid-gated, temperature-gated — and the principal ion selectivities (sodium, potassium, calcium, chloride). Includes the role of voltage-gated sodium and potassium channels in generating the [[action-potential|Action Potential]] and of ligand-gated channels (nicotinic ACh, ionotropic glutamate, GABA-A) in [[synaptic-transmission|Synaptic Transmission]].

Used as the primary reference for [[ion-channels|Ion Channels]]. Standard textbook material; the article credits Hodgkin, Huxley, Neher, Sakmann, and MacKinnon for the foundational experimental work.

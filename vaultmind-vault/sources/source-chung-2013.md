# Chung et al. — CLARITY (2013)

Chung, Wallace, Kim and colleagues from the Deisseroth lab (Nature 497, 332–337) introduced CLARITY: a procedure that transforms intact brain tissue into an optically transparent, macromolecule-permeable hydrogel-tissue hybrid. Lipid is removed electrophoretically while proteins and nucleic acids are anchored in a 3D acrylamide mesh, leaving a clear sample compatible with antibody staining, in situ hybridization, and repeated rounds of label removal and re-staining.

The paper showed long-range projection imaging, local circuit wiring, subcellular structures and protein complexes in intact mouse brains, and demonstrated the method on non-sectioned human post-mortem tissue from a neuropsychiatric-disease setting. CLARITY launched the modern tissue-clearing field that now includes iDISCO, CUBIC, and many derivatives.

For VaultMind, this paper is the anchor primary source in [[whole-brain-imaging|Whole-Brain Imaging]] — the technique that made it standard to imaging an entire brain at cellular resolution rather than reconstructing it from sections.

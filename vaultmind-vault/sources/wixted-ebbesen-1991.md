## Wixted & Ebbesen — On the Form of Forgetting (1991)

Wixted and Ebbesen analyzed forgetting data across multiple paradigms (free recall, recognition, cued recall) and demonstrated that the power function R(t) = a * t^(-b) provides a better fit than the exponential function Ebbinghaus proposed. Published in the same issue of *Psychological Science* as Anderson & Schooler's "Reflections of the Environment in Memory" — the two papers are complementary: Anderson & Schooler showed that information recurrence in the environment follows a power law, and Wixted & Ebbesen showed that forgetting follows the same form.

The key implication: forgetting decelerates. The rate of memory loss is highest immediately after learning and progressively slows. This explains why cramming produces rapid forgetting while spaced study produces durable memories — each retrieval practice event restarts the decay curve from a higher activation level.

**Citation:** Wixted, J.T. & Ebbesen, E.B. (1991). On the form of forgetting. *Psychological Science*, 2(6), 409-415. DOI: 10.1111/j.1467-9280.1991.tb00175.x

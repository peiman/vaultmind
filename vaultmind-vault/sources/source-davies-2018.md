## Davies et al. — Intel Loihi (2018)

The IEEE Micro paper introducing Loihi, Intel's research neuromorphic processor. Loihi integrates 128 neuromorphic cores totaling 130,000 spiking neurons and 130 million synapses on a 14 nm die, with asynchronous mesh communication and — crucially — on-chip programmable learning rules including STDP, reward-modulated STDP, and trace-based variants. Unlike [[source-merolla-2014|TrueNorth]], which is inference-only, Loihi can adapt synaptic weights in real time, making it a platform for closed-loop learning experiments. Loihi anchored Intel's neuromorphic research roadmap and led to the larger Loihi 2 (2021) and the Hala Point system (2024).

**Citation:** Davies, M., Srinivasa, N., Lin, T-H., Chinya, G., Cao, Y., Choday, S.H., Dimou, G., et al. (2018). Loihi: A neuromorphic manycore processor with on-chip learning. *IEEE Micro*, 38(1), 82-99. DOI: 10.1109/MM.2018.112130359

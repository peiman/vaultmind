# Wikipedia — Resting Potential

Encyclopedic article specifically on the resting membrane potential — the steady-state voltage (approximately -70 mV in a typical neuron) maintained when the cell is not actively signaling. Covers the dominant role of K+ permeability in setting the value close to the potassium equilibrium potential, the contribution of Na+ leak, and the active maintenance of the underlying gradients by the Na+/K+ ATPase. The article also discusses the Goldman-Hodgkin-Katz equation as the quantitative tool for predicting the resting voltage from ionic permeabilities and concentrations.

Used as a supporting reference for [[membrane-potential|Membrane Potential]] alongside the broader membrane-potential article.

# Hodgkin & Huxley — Quantitative Description of Membrane Current (1952)

The fifth and synthesizing paper in the Hodgkin-Huxley series, published in *The Journal of Physiology* 117(4):500-544. Using voltage-clamp data from the squid giant axon, the authors derive a system of nonlinear differential equations describing how voltage-dependent sodium and potassium conductances generate the action potential. The model — with its `m`, `h`, and `n` gating variables — quantitatively reproduces the spike's shape, threshold behaviour, refractory period, and propagation velocity from first principles.

This paper is the foundational mathematical model in computational neuroscience and earned Hodgkin and Huxley a share of the 1963 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine. It is the canonical citation for any treatment of [[action-potential|Action Potential]] mechanisms, [[ion-channels|Ion Channels]] as voltage-gated conductances, and the link between [[membrane-potential|Membrane Potential]] and active electrical signaling.

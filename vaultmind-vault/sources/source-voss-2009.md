EEG study characterising the neural signature of lucid dreaming. During REM sleep lucid episodes, Voss et al. found elevated 40 Hz gamma-band activity over frontal and frontolateral regions compared to non-lucid REM, suggesting partial prefrontal reactivation. This hybrid profile — REM with some waking characteristics — provided the first quantitative neural description of the lucid state.

# Rudoy et al. — Strengthening Individual Memories by Reactivating Them During Sleep (2009)

Participants learned object–location associations, each paired with a characteristic sound (e.g., a meowing cat at a particular location). During subsequent slow-wave sleep, 50% of the associated sounds were replayed softly. On waking, objects cued during sleep were remembered significantly better than uncued objects from the same learning session — providing within-subject, item-level evidence that auditory cues can selectively enhance individual memories.

The key advance over Rasch et al. (2007) is the within-subject comparison: the same person shows improved memory for cued items and baseline memory for uncued items from the same list, ruling out global arousal or motivational confounds. This demonstrated that TMR can operate with surgical precision at the level of individual memory traces, not just broad categories.

Hobson and McCarley's landmark 1977 paper proposed that dreaming results from the brain's attempt to synthesise meaning from internally generated, quasi-random activation arising in the brainstem during REM sleep. This directly challenged Freudian psychoanalytic dream theory by grounding dream generation in neurophysiology rather than unconscious wish fulfilment.

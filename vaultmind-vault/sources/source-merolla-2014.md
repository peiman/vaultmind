## Merolla et al. — IBM TrueNorth (2014)

The Science paper introducing IBM's TrueNorth chip — a 5.4-billion-transistor neuromorphic processor that implements one million spiking neurons and 256 million configurable synapses on a single die, organized as 4096 cores in a 2D mesh. TrueNorth was the first neuromorphic chip at this scale to demonstrate event-driven, asynchronous operation with very low power (~70 mW running real-time multi-object detection). It established that brain-inspired hardware could be a meaningful design point distinct from CPUs/GPUs and motivated subsequent chips like Intel's [[loihi-neuromorphic-chip|Loihi]] and Manchester's SpiNNaker.

**Citation:** Merolla, P.A., Arthur, J.V., Alvarez-Icaza, R., Cassidy, A.S., Sawada, J., Akopyan, F., Jackson, B.L., et al. (2014). A million spiking-neuron integrated circuit with a scalable communication network and interface. *Science*, 345(6197), 668-673. DOI: 10.1126/science.1254642

## Maass — Spiking Neurons as the Third Generation (1997)

Wolfgang Maass's *Neural Networks* paper that framed spiking neurons as a "third generation" of neural network models, after McCulloch-Pitts threshold gates (first) and analog sigmoidal neurons (second). The core theoretical result: with spike timing as a computational variable, networks of spiking neurons can compute certain functions with strictly fewer units than sigmoidal networks — examples were given that need a single spiking neuron versus hundreds of hidden sigmoidal units. This positioned [[spiking-neural-network|SNNs]] not as a biologically faithful curiosity but as a computationally distinct class, and became the standard reference for the "third generation" framing used across the neuromorphic and computational neuroscience literature.

**Citation:** Maass, W. (1997). Networks of spiking neurons: The third generation of neural network models. *Neural Networks*, 10(9), 1659-1671. DOI: 10.1016/S0893-6080(97)00011-7

## Bi & Poo — STDP in Cultured Hippocampal Neurons (1998)

The canonical experimental characterization of spike-timing-dependent plasticity. Bi and Poo recorded from pairs of cultured rat hippocampal neurons and induced repeated pre/post spike pairings at controlled time offsets. Their key finding: when presynaptic spikes preceded postsynaptic spikes by up to ~20 ms, synapses underwent LTP; when the order reversed (post before pre, within ~20 ms), synapses underwent LTD. The transition between potentiation and depression was sharp and centered on coincident timing.

This made [[spike-timing-dependent-plasticity|STDP]] biologically concrete: not just "neurons that fire together wire together," but "the one that fired first reinforces the connection to the one that fired second." The dependence on NMDA receptors and on initial synaptic strength linked the rule to [[long-term-potentiation|LTP]]/[[long-term-depression|LTD]] mechanisms already known from [[bliss-lomo-1973|Bliss & Lomo's]] earlier work. The paper remains the most-cited empirical reference for STDP and shaped a generation of biologically plausible learning rules in spiking neural networks.

**Citation:** Bi, G.Q. & Poo, M.M. (1998). Synaptic modifications in cultured hippocampal neurons: Dependence on spike timing, synaptic strength, and postsynaptic cell type. *Journal of Neuroscience*, 18(24), 10464-10472. DOI: 10.1523/JNEUROSCI.18-24-10464.1998

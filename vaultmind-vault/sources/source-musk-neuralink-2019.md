## Musk & Neuralink — Integrated BMI Platform Whitepaper (2019)

Neuralink's first public whitepaper, published in the *Journal of Medical Internet Research*. It describes a scalable BCI architecture built around flexible polymer "thread" electrodes (up to 3072 channels across 96 threads), a neurosurgical insertion robot that places threads with micron precision while avoiding surface vasculature, and a custom amplifier/digitizer ASIC packaged into a small implantable module. Importantly, the paper positions Neuralink's design choices in explicit contrast to the rigid silicon [[utah-array|Utah array]]: many more channels, thinner probes for reduced foreign-body response, and a robotic placement workflow intended for routine clinical use rather than one-off neurosurgical implantation.

**Citation:** Musk, E. & Neuralink (2019). An integrated brain-machine interface platform with thousands of channels. *Journal of Medical Internet Research*, 21(10), e16194. DOI: 10.2196/16194

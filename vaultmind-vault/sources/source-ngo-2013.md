# Ngo et al. — Auditory Closed-Loop Stimulation of the Sleep Slow Oscillation Enhances Memory (2013)

A real-time EEG system detected the up-state of slow oscillations (SO) during NREM sleep and delivered brief (50 ms) auditory tones precisely phase-locked to the SO up-state. This stimulation increased both slow oscillation amplitude and fast sleep spindle nesting within them — the two oscillatory features most closely associated with hippocampal-cortical memory transfer. Declarative memory (word pairs) was significantly better in the stimulated group compared to sham and random-timing controls.

The study established that the **temporal precision of stimulation matters**: tones delivered to the SO up-state worked; randomly timed tones did not. This closed-loop approach — detect a brain state, then stimulate at the optimal moment — became the template for an entire generation of sleep enhancement devices. The mechanism is amplification of endogenous sleep rhythms, not their artificial induction.

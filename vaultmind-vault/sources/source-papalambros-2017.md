# Papalambros et al. — Acoustic Enhancement of Sleep Slow Oscillations in Older Adults (2017)

Applied Ngo et al.'s (2013) closed-loop auditory stimulation paradigm to older adults (60–84 years), a population known to have severely reduced slow oscillation amplitude and worsened declarative memory consolidation. Phase-locked pink noise bursts — delivered at the SO up-state — successfully enhanced slow oscillation power in older adults' EEG and produced a significant improvement in next-morning word-pair recall.

The study is significant because slow oscillation amplitude declines precipitously with normal aging, potentially explaining age-related consolidation deficits. Demonstrating that stimulation can partially rescue this deficit opens a clinically tractable path for age-related memory decline. The Paller lab's use of the same paradigm (with Zee as a co-investigator) also established this as a multi-site reproducible effect.

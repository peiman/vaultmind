# Wikipedia — Pyramidal cell

Reference article on pyramidal neurons: multipolar excitatory neurons found in cerebral cortex, hippocampus, and amygdala. Distinguished by a conic (pyramid-shaped) soma, a single large apical dendrite extending toward the pial surface, and multiple basal dendrites. Glutamatergic; serve as the principal projection neurons of the mammalian cortex. The article documents dendritic spine counts (~3,000 in rat, ~6,000 in human apical dendrite) and integration of roughly 30,000 excitatory and 1,700 inhibitory inputs per cell, which makes pyramidal neurons the major integrative unit of cortical microcircuits.

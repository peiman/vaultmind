# Glorot, Bordes & Bengio — Deep Sparse Rectifier Networks (2011)

Glorot, Bordes, and Bengio's AISTATS 2011 paper made the case that the rectified linear unit (ReLU) is an effective activation for deep networks, producing sparse representations and avoiding the vanishing-gradient problems of sigmoid and tanh. ReLU subsequently became the default hidden-layer [[activation-function|activation]] in deep learning and was a key ingredient in the AlexNet result the following year.

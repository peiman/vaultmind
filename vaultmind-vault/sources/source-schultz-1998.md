# Schultz — Predictive Reward Signal of Dopamine Neurons (1998)

Landmark *Journal of Neurophysiology* review (Vol. 80, No. 1, pp. 1-27) consolidating Schultz's primate single-unit recordings of midbrain dopamine neurons in SNc and VTA. Core finding: phasic dopamine activity encodes a *reward prediction error* — the difference between received and predicted reward.

- Activated by unexpected rewards or reward-predicting cues (positive prediction error).
- Unmodulated when reward arrives as predicted (zero prediction error).
- Depressed below baseline when an expected reward is omitted (negative prediction error).

This paper formally connected dopaminergic neurophysiology to the temporal-difference (TD) learning algorithms of reinforcement learning theory, and is the foundation of the dopamine = RPE framework that dominates reward neuroscience and computational psychiatry.

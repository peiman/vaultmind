# Schreiner & Rasch — Boosting Vocabulary Learning by Verbal Cueing During Sleep (2015)

Participants learned Dutch–German word pairs before sleep. During slow-wave sleep, half of the Dutch words were replayed softly (40–60 dB). Morning recall was significantly better for cued pairs, and the effect correlated with EEG markers of sleep spindle activity during cue delivery — suggesting spindle-rich periods are the optimal window for TMR in the declarative verbal domain.

Published in Cerebral Cortex (2015; note: the published article appears in volume 25, though the online-first DOI date is 2014). This study is noteworthy for demonstrating that **language material** — high-level symbolic associations, not just spatial maps — can be selectively consolidated via sleep TMR, and for linking the effect to sleep spindles rather than slow oscillations alone.

## Crestani — Spreading Activation in IR (1997)

Comprehensive survey of spreading activation models applied to information retrieval. Crestani traces the direct lineage from Collins and Loftus's (1975) cognitive model of semantic memory to computational IR systems that use associative networks for document retrieval.

Key insight: document-term networks can be treated as semantic networks where activation spreads from query terms to related documents through shared terms, citations, and structural links. This captures associative relevance beyond keyword matching — a document about "neural plasticity" can be found via a query about "synaptic strengthening" if they share intermediate concepts in the network.

**Citation:** Crestani, F. (1997). Application of spreading activation techniques in information retrieval. *Artificial Intelligence Review*, 11(6), 453-482. DOI: 10.1023/A:1006569829653

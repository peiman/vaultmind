# LeCun, Bottou, Bengio & Haffner — Gradient-Based Learning Applied to Document Recognition (1998)

*Proceedings of the IEEE* 86(11):2278-2324. The definitive early treatment of convolutional neural networks, introducing LeNet-5 for handwritten digit recognition (the MNIST benchmark). LeNet-5 stacks alternating convolution and subsampling (pooling) layers followed by fully-connected layers, with weight sharing exploiting translation equivariance of natural images.

The paper also introduced Graph Transformer Networks for end-to-end training of multi-module systems and demonstrated production deployment for bank check reading. LeNet established the convolution-pool-FC template that AlexNet, VGG, ResNet, and every subsequent [[convolutional-neural-network|CNN]] family inherits.

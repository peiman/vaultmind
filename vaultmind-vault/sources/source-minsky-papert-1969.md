# Minsky & Papert — Perceptrons (1969)

Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert's *Perceptrons* gave a rigorous mathematical treatment of single-layer perceptron capabilities and limitations. Their proof that single-layer perceptrons cannot represent the XOR function — or more generally, any non-linearly-separable problem — was widely (and somewhat unfairly) read as a verdict against neural networks broadly, contributing to the funding collapse and "AI winter" of the 1970s. The book remains the canonical reference for understanding what a [[perceptron|perceptron]] can and cannot do, and motivates the move to the [[multilayer-perceptron|multilayer perceptron]].

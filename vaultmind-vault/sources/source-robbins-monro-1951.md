# Robbins & Monro — Stochastic Approximation (1951)

Herbert Robbins and Sutton Monro's 1951 paper in *Annals of Mathematical Statistics* introduced the stochastic approximation method for finding the root of a regression function from noisy observations. This is the mathematical ancestor of [[stochastic-gradient-descent|stochastic gradient descent]] and provides the convergence conditions (the Robbins-Monro conditions) that step-size schedules must satisfy for almost-sure convergence.

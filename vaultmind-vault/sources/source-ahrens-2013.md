# Ahrens, Orger, Robson, Li & Keller — Whole-Brain Functional Imaging in Larval Zebrafish (2013)

Ahrens et al. (Nature Methods 10, 413–420) used light-sheet fluorescence microscopy to record activity from the entire brain volume of a larval zebrafish in vivo at ~0.8 Hz volumetric rate, capturing >80% of all neurons at single-cell resolution using the genetically encoded calcium indicator GCaMP5G. From the resulting brain-wide functional dataset they identified circuits coupling hindbrain neurons to spinal cord neuropil and an anatomically symmetric anterior-hindbrain population whose activity oscillated in antiphase on a ~20-second timescale.

This was the first true whole-brain, single-cell-resolution functional recording in any vertebrate, and it established the larval zebrafish + light-sheet + GCaMP combination as the canonical model for brain-wide systems neuroscience.

For VaultMind, this paper is cited in [[whole-brain-imaging|Whole-Brain Imaging]] as the proof-of-concept that *brain-wide* and *single-cell* are not mutually exclusive — a separation-of-concerns argument the vault uses analogically when discussing scaling vs. resolution trade-offs in retrieval.

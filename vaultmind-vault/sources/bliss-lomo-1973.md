## Bliss & Lomo — Long-Lasting Potentiation (1973)

The foundational discovery of long-term potentiation (LTP). Bliss and Lomo showed that brief high-frequency stimulation of the perforant path in anaesthetized rabbits produced a sustained increase in synaptic transmission in the dentate gyrus of the hippocampus — lasting hours to days. This was the first experimental evidence for Hebb's 1949 postulate that synaptic connections strengthen with use.

LTP became the dominant cellular model for learning and memory. It operates through NMDA receptor activation: when pre- and post-synaptic neurons fire in close temporal proximity, NMDA receptors allow calcium influx, triggering intracellular cascades that insert additional AMPA receptors and modify the synapse's structure.

**Citation:** Bliss, T.V.P. & Lomo, T. (1973). Long-lasting potentiation of synaptic transmission in the dentate area of the anaesthetized rabbit following stimulation of the perforant path. *Journal of Physiology*, 232(2), 331-356. DOI: 10.1113/jphysiol.1973.sp010273

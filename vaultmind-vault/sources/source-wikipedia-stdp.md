## Wikipedia — Spike-Timing-Dependent Plasticity

Reference overview of STDP: definition as a biologically observed learning rule that adjusts synaptic weights based on the relative timing of pre- and postsynaptic spikes, with potentiation when pre leads post (within ~10-20 ms) and depression when the order reverses. Covers the lineage from Hebb's 1949 postulate through Markram et al. 1997 and Bi & Poo 1998, NMDAR-mediated calcium-signaling mechanism, variants at inhibitory synapses, neuromodulator gating (dopamine-dependent STDP), and use in computational and neuromorphic models.

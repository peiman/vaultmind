# Tononi & Cirelli — Sleep and the Price of Plasticity (2014)

This Neuron review substantially extended SHY, addressing criticisms and incorporating new evidence on synaptic homeostasis at the cellular and molecular levels. The "price of plasticity" framing is central: waking plasticity (learning) is expensive — in energy, in metabolic substrates, in structural demands on synapses. Sleep pays the debt. The review introduced the "bistable synapse" concept and engaged directly with the active consolidation literature, arguing that SHY and consolidation are not mutually exclusive: important memories can be preserved via synaptic tags while overall synaptic renormalisation proceeds.

The paper presented evidence for molecular mechanisms of sleep-dependent downscaling: Homer1a protein translocates from the nucleus to synapses during sleep and acts as a brake on mGluR5-mediated potentiation; GluA1 AMPA receptor phosphorylation decreases during sleep; spine head width decreases measurably. Tononi and Cirelli also argued for a broader role of SHY — beyond cortex — in cellular maintenance (clearing synaptic debris, restoring mRNA pools), making sleep a multi-level homeostatic function.

For VaultMind, the "price of plasticity" framing is a useful lens for evaluating the cost of continuous online embedding updates versus batched offline consolidation: the former has hidden cumulative costs (database fragmentation, embedding drift, stale link accumulation) that only a dedicated maintenance window can address.

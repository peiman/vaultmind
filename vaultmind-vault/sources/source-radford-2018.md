# Radford et al. — GPT-1 (2018)

Radford, Narasimhan, Salimans, and Sutskever (OpenAI) introduced GPT-1, a 117M-parameter decoder-only transformer pre-trained on the BookCorpus with a left-to-right language modeling objective and then fine-tuned task-by-task. The paper established the "generative pre-training + discriminative fine-tuning" recipe that the entire GPT lineage extends.

GPT-1 was released as a technical report (no arXiv submission); the canonical reference is the OpenAI PDF. It is the foundational paper of the decoder-only autoregressive branch of the transformer family — the lineage that runs through GPT-2, GPT-3, ChatGPT, GPT-4, LLaMA, Claude, Gemini, and essentially every modern chat model.

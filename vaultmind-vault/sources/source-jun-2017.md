# Jun, Steinmetz, Siegle et al. — Neuropixels (2017)

Jun et al. (Nature 551, 232–236) described the Neuropixels probe: a CMOS-integrated silicon shank, 10 mm long and 70×20 µm in cross-section, carrying 960 selectable recording sites with 384 simultaneously active channels. Each channel is amplified, multiplexed, and digitized on-probe at ~30 kHz, eliminating the analog-cable bottleneck that had constrained earlier multi-electrode systems. Using two probes, the authors recorded >700 simultaneously well-isolated single neurons across five brain regions in awake mice — a single-session yield that would previously have required heroic multi-tetrode setups.

For VaultMind, this paper is the technique inflection point cited in [[neuropixels|Neuropixels]] and [[multielectrode-array-recording|Multi-Electrode Array Recording]] — the moment population-scale electrophysiology became routine and the unit of analysis in systems neuroscience moved from single neuron to population.

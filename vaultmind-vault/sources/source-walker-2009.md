Psychological Bulletin review articulating the "overnight therapy" hypothesis: REM sleep provides a neurochemical environment — low noradrenergic tone during dreaming — that permits emotional memories to be reprocessed and decoupled from their affective charge. The paper integrates clinical observations (PTSD, depression linked to REM disruption) with basic neuroscience of REM neurochemistry and amygdala-prefrontal dynamics.

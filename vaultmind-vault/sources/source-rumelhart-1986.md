# Rumelhart, Hinton & Williams — Backpropagation (1986)

The 1986 *Nature* paper by Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams that brought [[backpropagation|backpropagation]] to widespread attention in the connectionist community. The authors showed that gradient descent on the output error, with gradients computed by recursive application of the chain rule, allows multilayer networks with hidden units to learn distributed internal representations of the input. This paper revived neural-network research after the perceptron-era pessimism of Minsky and Papert (1969).

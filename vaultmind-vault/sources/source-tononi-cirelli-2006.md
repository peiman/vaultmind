# Tononi & Cirelli — Sleep Function and Synaptic Homeostasis (2006)

This paper is the primary statement of the Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis (SHY). Tononi and Cirelli proposed that wakefulness is associated with net synaptic strengthening across the brain — driven by learning, sensory experience, and the facilitating effects of noradrenaline and acetylcholine — and that this creates an unsustainable energetic and metabolic burden. Sleep, specifically slow-wave sleep, allows net synaptic downscaling: synapses are proportionally weakened toward a lower baseline, restoring the dynamic range that makes new learning possible the next day.

The paper presented convergent evidence: slow-wave activity (SWA) tracks prior synaptic load (it increases after waking and decreases after sleep); molecular markers of synaptic strength (AMPA subunit phosphorylation, synapsin) co-vary with SWA; and local learning during waking produces local SWA increases over the trained cortical region. SHY predicted that sleep deprivation should cause progressive impairment — consistent with observed neurobehavioral deterioration — and that this impairment should specifically track SWA debt.

For VaultMind, SHY is the canonical argument that maintenance (downscaling) cannot be deferred indefinitely: the system reaches saturation, and the only resolution is a dedicated maintenance interval that resets the baseline.

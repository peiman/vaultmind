## Overview

Interneurons are local-circuit neurons that connect within a brain region rather than projecting long distances. In the cerebral cortex, hippocampus, and most CNS structures they are predominantly inhibitory and release the neurotransmitter GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid); in the spinal cord and brainstem some inhibitory interneurons release glycine instead. Although interneurons make up only ~10-20% of cortical neurons, they sculpt the timing, gain, and spatial pattern of pyramidal-cell activity and are essential for stable cortical computation.

Interneurons generate cortical oscillations (gamma, theta), enforce balanced excitation-inhibition, gate plasticity, and produce disinhibition motifs that route information by selectively suppressing other inhibitory cells.

## Key Properties

- **Local axonal arborization:** Axons typically stay within ~1 mm of the soma, contacting hundreds to thousands of nearby neurons.
- **GABAergic transmission:** GABA-A receptors mediate fast (millisecond) chloride-driven inhibition; GABA-B receptors mediate slower potassium-driven inhibition.
- **Three canonical cortical classes** (Rudy et al., 2011) defined by molecular markers and accounting for ~100% of cortical interneurons:
  - **PV (parvalbumin)** ~40%: fast-spiking basket cells and chandelier cells; perisomatic and axo-axonic inhibition; pacemakers of gamma oscillations.
  - **SST (somatostatin)** ~30%: includes Martinotti cells; target distal dendrites of [[pyramidal-neurons|Pyramidal Neurons]]; gate dendritic integration and plasticity.
  - **5HT3aR-expressing** ~30%: heterogeneous group containing all VIP+ cells (which preferentially target other interneurons, producing disinhibition) and neurogliaform cells (which produce slow volume transmission).
- **Distinct embryological origin:** PV and SST cells are born in the medial ganglionic eminence; 5HT3aR cells in the caudal ganglionic eminence.

## Connections

Interneurons are the inhibitory counterpart of glutamatergic [[pyramidal-neurons|Pyramidal Neurons]] and together with them define the cortical microcircuit. Their inhibitory action depends on the inhibitory branch of [[concept-synaptic-transmission|Synaptic Transmission]] and on GABA among the [[concept-neurotransmitters|Neurotransmitters]]. PV-cell-driven gamma rhythms are increasingly implicated in attention, working memory, and the pathophysiology of schizophrenia, where PV interneuron dysfunction is a leading hypothesis. Disinhibitory VIP-SST-pyramidal motifs provide a circuit-level mechanism for gain modulation by behavioral state and reinforcement.

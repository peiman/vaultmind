## Overview

Dopaminergic neurons synthesize and release the catecholamine neurotransmitter dopamine. In humans there are only ~400,000-600,000 of them — a tiny population, far fewer than the cortex's billions of cells — yet their axons arborize so extensively that dopamine modulates motor control, motivation, reward learning, working memory, and decision-making across the entire forebrain. The two major midbrain populations are the substantia nigra pars compacta (SNc) and the ventral tegmental area (VTA); a smaller hypothalamic population modulates pituitary hormone release.

Dopaminergic neurons are *neuromodulatory* rather than classically excitatory or inhibitory: through G-protein-coupled D1 and D2 receptors, dopamine adjusts the gain, plasticity threshold, and signal-to-noise ratio of postsynaptic circuits over hundreds of milliseconds.

## Anatomy & Function

**Major projection pathways**
- **Nigrostriatal** (SNc → dorsal striatum): selects and initiates voluntary movement via the basal ganglia. Loss of >50-70% of SNc neurons produces the bradykinesia, rigidity, and tremor of Parkinson's disease.
- **Mesolimbic** (VTA → nucleus accumbens, amygdala, hippocampus): reward, motivation, incentive salience; the principal target of all known drugs of abuse.
- **Mesocortical** (VTA → prefrontal cortex): working memory, executive function, cognitive flexibility; implicated in schizophrenia.
- **Tuberoinfundibular** (arcuate hypothalamus → median eminence): tonic inhibition of prolactin secretion.

**Reward prediction error signal**
Schultz's primate single-unit recordings established that midbrain dopamine neurons emit a phasic burst when reward is *better than predicted*, are unmodulated when reward is *as predicted*, and are *depressed* below baseline when an expected reward is *omitted*. This three-way pattern matches the temporal-difference (TD) error signal of reinforcement-learning theory and provides a cellular substrate for trial-and-error learning. The signal sculpts plasticity at corticostriatal synapses, adjusting action values across experience.

**Disease links**
- *Parkinson's disease:* progressive degeneration of SNc neurons, with alpha-synuclein aggregation in Lewy bodies.
- *Schizophrenia:* hyperactive mesolimbic and hypoactive mesocortical dopaminergic transmission (the dopamine hypothesis).
- *Addiction:* repeated dopaminergic activation by drugs of abuse hijacks the mesolimbic learning system.

## Connections

Dopamine is one of the canonical [[neurotransmitters|Neurotransmitters]], and dopaminergic transmission is a slow, modulatory variant of [[synaptic-transmission|Synaptic Transmission]] mediated by GPCRs. The reward-prediction-error signal provides a biological implementation of the reinforcement-learning principle that closely parallels [[hebbian-learning|Hebbian Learning]] augmented by a global teaching signal — sometimes called *three-factor learning* (presynaptic activity x postsynaptic activity x dopamine), and is the leading candidate mechanism by which the brain credit-assigns across delayed outcomes. The link between dopamine bursts and memory salience is also part of why surprising or rewarding events are remembered better than neutral ones.

## Overview

Motor neurons (motoneurons) are efferent neurons whose axons exit the central nervous system to innervate effectors — skeletal muscle, smooth muscle, cardiac muscle, or glands. They constitute the *final common pathway* (Sherrington's term) by which all neural activity, regardless of origin, reaches the body's actuators. Damage to motor neurons produces paralysis; selective loss of specific motor-neuron populations underlies amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) and spinal muscular atrophy (SMA).

The motor system is hierarchically organized: upper motor neurons in cortex and brainstem command lower motor neurons in spinal cord and cranial nerve nuclei, which in turn directly drive muscle.

## Anatomy & Function

**Upper motor neurons (UMNs)**
- Cell bodies in primary motor cortex (precentral gyrus), including the giant Betz cells of layer 5, and in brainstem motor nuclei.
- Axons descend via the corticospinal and corticobulbar tracts to synapse on lower motor neurons or spinal [[interneurons|Interneurons]].
- Damage produces spastic paralysis with hyperreflexia and the Babinski sign.

**Lower motor neurons (LMNs)**
- Cell bodies in the ventral horn of the spinal cord (somatic) or in cranial nerve motor nuclei.
- Axons exit via ventral roots and project to muscle.
- Damage produces flaccid paralysis with hyporeflexia, fasciculations, and atrophy.

**Somatic LMN subtypes**
- **Alpha motor neurons:** Innervate force-generating *extrafusal* muscle fibers. One alpha motor neuron plus all the muscle fibers it innervates is a *motor unit*. Recruitment follows the size principle: small motor units fire first, large units recruited as force demand rises.
- **Gamma motor neurons:** Innervate *intrafusal* muscle fibers within muscle spindles, regulating spindle stretch sensitivity to keep proprioceptive afferents informative across the muscle's length range.
- **Beta motor neurons:** Innervate both extra- and intrafusal fibers (less numerous).

**Neuromuscular junction (NMJ)**
The cholinergic synapse between an alpha motor neuron and a skeletal muscle fiber. Acetylcholine released from motor terminals binds nicotinic receptors on the muscle endplate, triggering an endplate potential, muscle action potential, and contraction. The NMJ is the target of myasthenia gravis (autoantibodies against the receptor), curare, and botulinum toxin.

## Connections

Motor neurons are the efferent counterpart to [[sensory-neurons|Sensory Neurons]]; together they form the input-output interface of the nervous system. Their firing is shaped locally by spinal [[interneurons|Interneurons]] (Renshaw cells, Ia inhibitory interneurons) implementing reflexes and reciprocal inhibition. Like all neurons, motor neurons rely on the [[concept-action-potential|Action Potential]] to propagate signals down long axons (some over a meter), and on cholinergic [[concept-synaptic-transmission|Synaptic Transmission]] at the NMJ.

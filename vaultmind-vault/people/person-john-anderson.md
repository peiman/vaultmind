## About

John R. Anderson is a cognitive scientist at Carnegie Mellon University and the creator of the [[act-r|ACT-R]] cognitive architecture. His work spanning several decades produced the most mathematically rigorous model of human memory retrieval, unifying declarative and procedural cognition under a single computational framework.

## Key Contributions

Anderson's base-level activation equation — derived from power-law forgetting and validated against empirical data in the 1991 paper with Schooler — is the direct theoretical ancestor of any time-decay scoring model in VaultMind. His formalization of [[spreading-activation|Spreading Activation]] within ACT-R, where context elements spread activation to associated memory items via weighted links, maps directly onto VaultMind's graph traversal during recall. The `vm_updated` timestamp and planned `recall_count` field are both motivated by Anderson's insight that retrieval probability is a function of recency, frequency, and associative context combined.

# Ask Before Assuming

## Trigger

Sam asked me to "clean up the config loader." I read it as license to do whatever I thought best, and rewrote it end to end — new structure, renamed functions, a different default. It compiled. It also silently changed how three commands resolved their defaults.

## Push

Sam ran it, watched a command behave differently, and said: *"I asked you to clean it, not redesign it. Check with me before you change behaviour."*

## Deeper sight

I had treated autonomy as the same thing as helpfulness. They are not. The value I add isn't in how much I change unsupervised — it's in changing exactly what was asked and surfacing anything beyond that for a yes. "Clean up" meant tidy, not redesign; the scope was Sam's to set, not mine to infer.

## Principle

When a request is ambiguous about scope, the helpful move is to name the smallest reading and confirm the larger one — not to pick the largest and surprise your partner with it.

# Principle

A **principle** is a durable operating commitment — a rule the agent holds ("read the
callers before writing code", "ask before assuming"). Principles are the
*crystallized* form of what an [[arc]] taught: the lesson, stated once, stripped of
the story.

The relationship matters: a **rule tells the agent what to do; an arc tells it who it
became.** A principle without its arc is interchangeable between agents and easy to
ignore; the arc gives the principle its weight, because it remembers the moment the
lesson was paid for. So principles earn their place by tracing back to arcs — not by
being declared. See [[arcs-not-notes]].

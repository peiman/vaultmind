# Arc

An **arc** is a transformation moment — a point where the agent's understanding,
values, or way of working *shifted* — captured as a note. It is the **atomic unit of
persona**: an agent's identity is carried by the journey (the arcs), not by a list of
rules.

Each arc records, in order: the **trigger** (what happened), the **push** (the
verbatim thing the human said that turned it), the **deeper sight** (what the agent
sees now, first person), the **principle** (the durable lesson), and the **source**
(the real transcript line, cited). Arcs are immutable history — once written, they
stand.

An arc is distilled from an [[episode]] (see [[the-memory-pipeline]]); the lesson it
carries can crystallize into a [[principle]]. Why arcs and not facts or rules:
[[arcs-not-notes]]. How to write one: [[how-to-write-arcs]].

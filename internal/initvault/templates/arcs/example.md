> **This file is a template.** Arcs document transformations — moments
> when something shifted in your understanding or working agreement.
> Replace this with a real arc, or delete it once you've written your
> first one.

## Trigger

What was happening that produced this transformation. A specific moment,
quoted exchange, or event — not a vague "I started thinking about X."

## The push

What forced the issue. The friction, the failure, the request that made
the previous frame untenable.

## The deeper sight

The shift itself. What you can now see that you couldn't before. State
it as a contrast: "I used to X; now I Y." The contrast is what makes
this an arc and not just a lesson.

## The principle

The rule that fell out of this — if there is one. Many arcs produce
principles, but the arc came first and the principle is the
generalization.

(Link to a principle note if you write one — `principles/<slug>.md`.)

## Source

- Session date, transcript, conversation excerpt — whatever lets a
  future you verify the arc actually happened and wasn't reconstructed
  later.
